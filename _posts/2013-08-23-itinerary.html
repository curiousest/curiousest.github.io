Aer Lingus flight from Dublin, Ireland > Heathrow Airport, London. Malaysian Airlines flight from Heathrow Airport, London > KLIA, Malaysia > Phuket Airport. Taxi from airport.

June 3 Watched fights live, including some students from our gym. (video of one)
June 7 Finished Muay Thai training. (unrelated blog post on sweating)

July 1 Arrived in Chiang Mai, Thailand.
Air Asia flight from LCCT > Chiang Mai Airport. Taxi from airport.
(map) | (1.5 months' acommodation) | (wikitravel)

July 2 Started learning Thai Massage at TMC School, five weeks of weekdays 9am-4pm. (website)
July 6 Visited the Saturday night market. I loved the night markets in Chiang Mai. Over the course of my stay, I went to the Saturday and Sunday night markets four times each. I also went to the Night Bazaar, Rincome Market, Chiang Mai University Gate Market, and the lunch market beside the massage school.
July 13 Petted tigers at Tiger Kingdom and got sick on Doi Suthep.
July 20 Went mountain biking down Doi Suthep. Fell ~20 times, mostly flipping over the handlebars.
July 27 Did a visa run to the Burma border to extend my visa for another 15 days.
August 2 Graduated from the "Professional Thai Massage 150 Hour Curriculum" at TMC School.
August 6 Visited a zoo. The enclosures there allowed you to get much closer to the animals. I also had never seen a mouse deer before.
August 10 Watched an elephant show and explored some waterfalls. I explored five different waterfalls, all around the Doi Suthep mountain.
August 13 Scaled Doi Suthep with a car to visit royal gardens, a coffee shop with a panoramic view, and a Thai hill tribe. I also got my passport back from applying for an Indian visa. Unfortunately, the visa application made me three days overstayed on my Thai visa. I was already leaving on the 18th, so I decided to risk five days more of overstaying and 2500THB (60EU) more in fines instead of doing another day-long border crossing.
August 16 Celebrated my birthday - video games, 6.5k run, and foie gras. I loved the 6.5k run around the old city moat, and did it 10-15 times during my stay. It was best at night when it was cool, full of activity, and lit up by light reflecting off the water.

August 19 Walked along the Mekong River at sunset. There's nothing to do in Vientiane.

October 7 I experienced the heaviest bout of culture shock / homesickness of my whole trip. It had started building up during my final weeks in Batu Karas, got worse in Jakarta, and took hold of me in Chennai. I would have made plans to leave the country if I didn't want to do the Vipassana course in Bangalore so resolutely. I fully recovered after the next day.
